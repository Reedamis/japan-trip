Jan 23
Minoh Falls hike + Solaniwa Onsen evening
🥾🛁
🥾
Minoh Falls (箕面大滝)
2.7km · Easy · Paved · Free
40 min from Osaka by Hankyu Minoh Line. Forested river valley trail to a 33m waterfall. Crisp and beautiful in January. Wild macaques in the park. Allow 2–2.5 hrs round trip.
🛁
Solaniwa Onsen (空庭温泉)
Stickers for communal baths Private kashikiri available
9 spring types fed by water from 1,000m underground. Rooftop Japanese garden, torii gate path. Book a private kashikiri bath (¥4,000–7,000/hr) to soak together tattoo-free. Open until 11pm. 10-min walk from Bentencho Station.
🍜 Food today
🍡
Minoh Park — maple leaf tempura from stalls along the trail. Sounds weird, tastes great.
🍜
Dinner — Kadoya Shokudo · 60+ year old Bib Gourmand institution. Classic shoyu ramen, thin noodles, bamboo shoots. Not fishy at all — great intro ramen. Get the gyoza.

🏙️
Morning — Dotonbori + Kuromon Market

Kinosaki Onsen
Jan 24–27  ·  3 nights  ·  All baths 100% tattoo-friendly
Jan 24
Arrive Kinosaki · first evening bath
🧣🛁
🧣
Check into ryokan — Morizuya Inn (recommended)
4.8 stars, family-owned for 8 generations, private cave onsen, perfectly central. morizuya.com — book early, peak crab season fills fast. Your ryokan provides yukata, wooden geta sandals, and a bathhouse pass. Put the yukata on immediately — everyone in town walks around in them.
🛁 Tonight — pick one bathhouse
1
Ichino Yu (一の湯) — Built into a cave face. Most dramatic first-night choice.
2
Satono Yu (さとの湯) — Most modern, largest facility, multiple bath types. Good if you want variety in one visit.
🦀 Tonight — ryokan kaiseki dinner
🦀
Peak Matsuba snow crab season — fished fresh from the nearby Sea of Japan port. Multi-course kaiseki dinner included with your ryokan stay. Eat in your yukata at a low table. This is one of the best things you can eat in Japan in winter.
Jan 25
Mt. Daishi ropeway + Onsenji Temple · evening bath
🥾⛩️🛁
🚡
Kinosaki Ropeway → Mt. Daishi summit
Panoramic views of the Sea of Japan and the entire Kinosaki valley. Take the ropeway up, walk the descent trail back down through mountain cedar forest.
⛩️
Onsenji Temple (温泉寺) — 500 stone steps
Ancient temple midway down the mountain, said to have founded Kinosaki's hot springs 1,300 years ago. 500 mossy stone steps winding through old-growth cedar. The steps earn the evening soak.
🏘️
Afternoon — canal wandering + food stalls
Walk the willow-lined canal in your yukata. Souvenir shops, grilled crab leg street stalls, small cafes. Optional day trip to Izushi samurai castle town (30 min bus) — snow-dusted old town and famous soba.
🛁 Tonight — Gosho no Yu (save for a special evening)
★
Gosho no Yu (御所の湯) — The crown jewel. Only fully all-outdoor rotenburo in Kinosaki — three-tiered pools facing two cascading waterfalls, open sky above. In February with cold air and steam rising this is the best soak of the trip. Closed Thursdays.
🦀 Food today
🦀
Grilled crab leg from a street stall while wandering back in your yukata. That's a very good 10 minutes.
🍱
Ryokan kaiseki dinner — second night of the full crab spread.
Jan 26
Final morning soak · slow morning · depart for Kyoto
🌅🚆
🌅
Morning bath — Mandara Yu or Yanagi Yu
Most bathhouses open at 7am. A morning soak in February — cold air, steam, nobody around — is the defining memory of Kinosaki. 30–40 minutes, get out, get warm, ryokan breakfast, pack.

Kyoto
Jan 26–30  ·  3 nights  ·  Temples · ramen · onsen
Jan 27
Arashiyama + Monkey Park + Funaoka Onsen
🎋🐒🛁
🎋
Arashiyama Bamboo Grove — go early
At 8–9am in late January you may have it nearly to yourselves. Towering green stalks in winter light. Combine with Tenryu-ji temple garden right next door.
🐒
Iwatayama Monkey Park
20-min steep hike up the mountain. At the top you're inside a wire-mesh hut — ~120 wild macaques roam freely outside and press against the mesh for treats. You hand food through the wire. Totally ethical, no chains, no performing. ¥800 each. Panoramic view of Kyoto below.
🛁
Funaoka Onsen (船岡温泉) — evening
Fully tattoo-friendly · No covering ⚠ Closed Tuesdays
Kyoto's most beloved tattoo-friendly sento. Built 1923, extraordinary wood carvings of historical battle scenes throughout the changing room. Multiple pools, cold plunge, outdoor bath, sauna. Opens 3pm weekdays (8am Sundays). ~¥550.
🍜 Food today
☕
% Arabica, Arashiyama — riverside location, stunning espresso. Best morning coffee of the trip.
🍱
Lunch — Omen Nanzenji · Handmade udon in rich broth with toppings on the side. Hearty, warming, a Kyoto institution.
🍜
Dinner — Kamodashi Chukasoba ROKU · Kyoto-style ramen by a Michelin-trained chef. Refined but hearty chicken broth. One of the best bowls in the city.
Jan 28
Kurama mountain hike + Kurama Hot Spring
🥾🛁
🚃
Eizan Railway → Kurama Station
From Demachiyanagi Station · ~30 min · ~¥430 each way · Pay with ICOCA — not JR covered.
⛩️
Kurama-dera Temple → over the ridge → Kibune
Moderate · 3–4 hrs · Possibly icy in January
Hike up through the ancient mountain temple, traverse the ridge over to the Kibune valley on the other side, then walk back to Kurama. Steep icy descent in January — great boots essential. The forest feels ancient and completely otherworldly in winter.
🛁
Kurama Hot Spring (くらま温泉)
Fully tattoo-friendly · No covering
Outdoor rotenburo in the mountain forest. Steam rises into cold mountain air. ¥2,500 for indoor + outdoor baths. Open until 7pm — time your hike to finish here. 5-min walk from Kurama Station.
🍜 Food today
🍡
Kibune village — small cozy restaurants along the river road. A bowl of noodles or tofu hotpot (yudofu) after the hike, before the onsen.
🍺
Evening — izakaya near Kyoto Station · Edamame, karaage, yakitori skewers, gyoza, cold Asahi. Look for a red lantern (akachochin) outside.
Jan 29
Slow Kyoto day · Nishiki Market · Philosopher's Path · ramen crawl
🏛️🍜
🏮
Nishiki Market (錦市場)
Kyoto's "kitchen" — a covered 400-year-old market street. Pickles, tofu, fresh yuba, grilled skewers, matcha everything. Go hungry, graze your way through.
🌿
Philosopher's Path (哲学の道)
2km canal-side walkway connecting Ginkaku-ji and Nanzen-ji. Quiet in January, small cafes and galleries along the way. Good afternoon wander.
⛩️
Fushimi Inari (optional — if not done yet)
Thousands of torii gates winding up a mountain. Go late afternoon when crowds thin. The upper sections in January are often misty and very atmospheric.
🍜 Full ramen day — Kyoto
🍜
Lunch — Menya Inoichi Hanare · Delicate but deeply flavourful chicken and clam broth. Unusual and memorable. One of Kyoto's most talked-about bowls.
🍜
Dinner — Takayasu · Old-school Kyoto-style shoyu ramen, been open since the 1940s. Dark soy broth, thin noodles, green onion. Deeply local, no frills.
🍡
Dessert — matcha soft serve anywhere on Nishiki. You'll see them.

Kumano Kodo + Yunomine Onsen
Jan 30–Feb 1  ·  2 nights  ·  Ancient forest trail · oldest onsen in Japan
Jan 30–31
Nakahechi trail — hike into Hongu
🌲🥾
🌲
Kumano Kodo — Nakahechi route
Moderate · 2-day walk · Ancient pilgrimage trail
A 1,000-year-old pilgrimage route through the mountains of the Kii Peninsula — the route emperors walked. Towering ancient cedars, moss-covered stone steps, river gorges, shrine gates appearing out of nowhere. The Kansai area stays green all winter. February means near-zero crowds on the trail. Luggage transfer service available — hike light.
🌸
February on the trail
Late January into February brings ume (plum) blossoms through the forest — white dapples in the cedars, considered in ancient times more important than cherry blossoms. The cold makes the forest feel otherworldly.
⛩️
Kumano Hongu Taisha — trail endpoint
One of Japan's most sacred shrines, deep in the mountains. Arrive on foot the way pilgrims have for a thousand years.
🍜 Food on the trail
🍱
Pack lunch — buy onigiri and a hot drink at a konbini before the trailhead. Small guesthouses (minshuku) along the route serve simple hearty dinners — rice, miso, mountain vegetables, grilled fish.
Feb 1
Yunomine Onsen · depart for Kobe/Arima
🛁🚆
🛁
Yunomine Onsen (湯の峰温泉)
Tattoo-friendly · check with your ryokan
One of the oldest hot springs in Japan — pilgrims have been purifying themselves here for over 1,000 years. A tiny village of perhaps 30 buildings. The public bath Tsuboyu is a UNESCO World Heritage listed onsen (the only one in the world). Morning soak, then breakfast, then head out.
🚆
Bus: Yunomine → Shingu Station · JR Kuroshio Limited Express: Shingu → Shin-Kobe · ~3 hrs · Covered by Kansai Wide Area Pass
Kobe → Arima Onsen
Feb 1–3  ·  2 nights
Feb 1
Arrive Kobe · Kitano + Nankinmachi
🏯🥟
🏯
Kitano Ijinkan district
Hillside district of 19th-century Western-style mansions built by foreign merchants. Strangely beautiful, very quiet in February.
🥟
Nankinmachi — Kobe's Chinatown
Compact and lively. Pork buns, dumplings, char siu from street stalls. Kobe is also famous for beef — if you want a proper dinner, Kobe beef teppanyaki here is more affordable than Tokyo.
🍜 Food today
🥩
Kobe beef teppanyaki — you're in Kobe, it would be criminal not to. Lunch is cheaper than dinner. Look for a set lunch around ¥3,000–5,000.
🍜
Ramen — Ramen Kokoro · Kobe-style thick soy broth, hearty and rich. Very different from Kyoto's refined bowls.
Feb 2
Mt. Rokko hike → descend into Arima Onsen
🥾🏔️🛁
🏔️
Mt. Rokko traverse (六甲山)
Moderate-Hard · 4–5 hrs · Chains · Possible frost
Start at Ashiyagawa trailhead (JR from Kobe ~10 min + bus ~¥230). Rocky sections with fixed chains, panoramic views of Kobe and Osaka Bay. Possible frost on the north-facing descent in February. The trail deposits you directly into Arima Onsen town. Most satisfying hike of the trip.
🛁
Kin no Yu — Golden Spring (金の湯)
Fully tattoo-friendly
Iron-rich, rust-brown mineral water. One of Japan's most distinctive hot springs. Arrive straight off the mountain. ~¥800. Open until 9pm.
🛁
Gin no Yu — Silver Spring (銀の湯)
Fully tattoo-friendly
Different mineral profile, lighter water. ~¥550. 10-min walk from Kin no Yu. Do both in the same evening if you have energy.
🏡
Ryokan overnight · private kashikiri bath
Soak together · No stickers · Book ahead
Negiya Ryofukaku or Gekkoen Korokan both offer private rental baths for tattooed guests — ¥3,000–5,000 per 45–60 min. Tatami rooms, yukata, kaiseki dinner. After the hike you will sleep like rocks.
🍜 Food today
🍱
Pack lunch — onigiri from a konbini before the hike. You'll be on the mountain all day.
🍱
Ryokan kaiseki dinner — multi-course traditional dinner included. Arima mountain vegetables, tofu, grilled fish, miso, rice. Eat in your yukata. This is the real deal.

🌅

Arima is a lovely little onsen town — a few hours of wandering the stone-paved streets, temples, and sake shops before heading back.

Osaka — Final Days
Feb 3–5  ·  2 nights  ·  Eat everything · depart Feb 5
Feb 3–4
Full food blitz · one last soak
🍜🛁
🍜
Ura-Namba ramen crawl
The back streets behind Namba Station — tiny ramen shops, izakayas, zero tourists. Wander until something looks right and go in. This is where Osaka people actually eat.
🥩
Yakiniku (grilled meat, Korean BBQ style)
Osaka has fantastic yakiniku. Wagyu gyutan (beef tongue) grilled at the table, cold beer. Great final dinner energy.
🍢
Shinsekai — kushikatsu
Deep-fried skewers dipped in communal brown sauce. Rule: dip once, never double-dip. Daruma is the classic chain. Get the beef, quail egg, lotus root, corn.
🛁
Optional — one last soak at Solaniwa or Irifune
Solaniwa: stickers for communal baths Irifune: fully tattoo-friendly
Solaniwa open until 11pm — book a private kashikiri bath and end the trip with a long soak together. Or Irifune for a cheap, local, no-fuss neighbourhood sento.
🍜 Final ramen picks — Osaka
🍜
Menya Yamahide · Spicy Taiwan ramen — minced pork, chili, chives. Bold and hearty. The most satisfying bowl on this list.
🍜
Zundo-Ya · Rich tonkotsu, multiple Osaka locations. Customize broth richness and noodle firmness. Stewed pork platter as a side is mandatory.
🥟
Gyoza Stadium, Namba Parks · Entire food court dedicated to gyoza from different regional styles across Japan. Order 6 kinds and share.

Essential notes
🎫
Kansai Wide Area Pass (5-day, ¥12,000/person) — buy two per person. Activate Pass #1 on Jan 24 (Kinosaki train). Activate Pass #2 on Jan 30 (Kumano train). Buy in advance via Klook or JR West official site before leaving Portland. Covers: all JR trains between cities, limited express to Kinosaki, Kuroshio to Kumano, and Kobe/Arima trains.
💳
ICOCA card — pick up at KIX on arrival. Load ¥10,000–15,000. Works on every subway, bus, local train, and most convenience stores. Use everywhere the JR pass doesn't cover (Eizan Railway to Kurama, city subways, local buses).
🛁
Tattoo summary: Solaniwa — stickers for communal baths, private kashikiri available. Healthy Spa Tateba + Irifune (Osaka), Funaoka + Kurama (Kyoto), Kin no Yu + Gin no Yu (Arima), all 6 Kinosaki bathhouses — fully tattoo-friendly, no covering needed.
👘
Kinosaki ryokan — book ASAP. Morizuya Inn (morizuya.com) — 4.8 stars, 8 generations family-owned, private cave onsen. February is peak crab season and fills months ahead. All ryokan include dinner + breakfast.
🥾
Hiking boots — Waterproof, mid-height, Vibram soles. Salomon X Ultra 5 Mid GTX (~$190) or Merrell Moab 3 Mid WP (~$130). Break them in well before January — 50+ miles minimum.
🧥
Cold weather — Osaka/Kyoto: 3–8°C. Kinosaki (Sea of Japan coast): 2–5°C with real snow possible. Kumano (Kii Peninsula): milder, 5–10°C, stays green. Pack a serious insulated jacket, thermal underlayers, hat, gloves, wool socks.
💴
Cash — Japan is largely cash-based outside major cities. Carry ¥20,000–30,000 at all times. 7-Eleven and Japan Post ATMs reliably accept foreign cards.
🍜
Ramen cheat sheet: Tonkotsu = rich pork bone broth (milky, hearty). Shoyu = soy sauce broth (lighter, savory, not fishy). Miso = fermented soybean broth (earthy, warming). Spicy tantanmen = sesame + chili (bold). Start with shoyu or tonkotsu — crowd-pleasing, not fishy.
