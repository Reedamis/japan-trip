Trip 1 — Alsace → Baden-Baden → Salzburg

The thermal corridor. Fairy-tale Alsatian wine villages and kougelhopf bakeries give way to Baden-Baden — one of Europe's great spa cities built on Roman hot springs — then east into Salzburg, Austria's most beautiful small city, ringed by Alpine hiking trails and a short train ride from the legendary Bad Gastein thermal resort.

Strasbourg
Friday–Saturday, Days 2–3 🌙🌙

Your base for Alsace. A canal city of half-timbered houses, a soaring Gothic cathedral, and warm bouchons on every corner. The old town — Grande Île — is a UNESCO-listed island surrounded by canals. January is cold and quiet, the Christmas market crowds are gone, and the city feels genuinely local.

✈️ Arriving via Basel airport + 1h train

🥐 Bakeries & Food

▼
The Bakeries
🥐
Kougelhopf

The Alsatian crown pastry. A ring-shaped brioche studded with raisins and almonds, dusted with powdered sugar. Every boulangerie makes one. Eat it warm, with butter, at the counter.

🥨
Bretzel

Alsace is the pretzel's homeland. Soft, chewy, proper lye crust and coarse salt. Pick one up from any boulangerie for the walk every morning.

🍞
Pain d'épices

Alsatian spiced gingerbread flavoured with anise, cinnamon, and honey. Found in every bakery and market stall.

Restaurants
🥗
Tarte flambée

Thin crispy dough, crème fraîche, lardons, onions. Order multiples — they're thin and addictive. The definitive bouchon dish.

🥬
Choucroute garnie

Alsatian sauerkraut piled with pork knuckle, sausages, and potatoes. A proper winter dish with a glass of Alsatian Riesling.

🗺️ Attractions

▼
⛪
Strasbourg Cathedral

One of the tallest medieval buildings in the world. The Gothic facade took 250 years to build. Climb to the platform for views across the Rhine plain into Germany.

🏘️
La Petite France

Half-timbered houses reflected in still canals, lock gates, weir walkways. Empty in January. The kind of place where you just stop and stand there.

🖼️
Musée Alsacien

Three connected 17th-century townhouses turned folk museum. Small, warm, and deeply local — perfect for a cold afternoon.

🛁 Baths

▼
♨️
Les Bains Municipaux

Strasbourg's historic municipal baths — an Art Nouveau building from 1908. Hot pools, steam rooms, locals-only vibe. Very affordable, zero pretension. Tattoo policy: relaxed.

♨️
Niederbronn-les-Bains

Roman-origin thermal springs 40 min north of Strasbourg. A small spa town built entirely around the baths. Quiet, genuine, almost no tourists in January.

🥾 Hiking

▼
⛰️
Vosges Mountains day hikes

The Vosges ridge runs directly west of Strasbourg. Round forested hills, clear trails, viewpoints over the Rhine plain, old castle ruins at the summits. January hiking is crisp and uncrowded — bring microspikes if there's been snow.

🏰
Haut-Kœnigsbourg Castle hike

Medieval castle on a Vosges summit at 755m. Combine the castle visit with a forest hike below. Views across the Rhine to the Black Forest on clear days.

Alsace Wine Villages
Sunday, Day 4 🌙 — Day Trip from Strasbourg

The Route des Vins d'Alsace runs south through a string of medieval wine villages so pretty they look fictional. Half-timbered houses, cobbled lanes, frost-quiet in January. Each village takes about an hour to walk properly — hit two or three in a day.

🚗 Rent a car for the day from Strasbourg — villages are 15–30 min apart along the route.

🗺️ Villages

▼
🏘️
Riquewihr

A walled village almost unchanged since the 16th century. No cars inside the walls. Wine shops and excellent pâtisseries. In January it is practically yours alone.

🏘️
Eguisheim

Built in concentric rings around a castle courtyard. One of France's officially designated most beautiful villages. The boulangerie here is exceptional.

🏘️
Kaysersberg

Ruined castle above, fortified bridge over the river, some of the best pâtisseries on the entire wine route. Good lunch stop.

🏙️
Colmar

The main hub of the route. "Little Venice" canal district, excellent covered market, more bakeries per block than seems reasonable.

🥐 Bakeries Along the Route

▼
🍰
Tarte aux quetsches

Alsatian plum tart — short pastry shell with preserved quetsche plums. Found in every village pâtisserie along the route.

🍪
Bredele

Small spiced Alsatian butter cookies — anise, cinnamon, almond. Still in stock in January. Buy a bag for the drive.

Baden-Baden
Monday–Tuesday, Days 5–6 🌙🌙

Technically Germany, but it fits so naturally into this arc that skipping it would be a mistake. Baden-Baden is one of Europe's great spa cities — built on Roman thermal springs in continuous use since the 1st century. Small, elegant, set in the Black Forest hills. Napoleon III came here. Brahms composed here. In January it's quiet, affordable, and exactly what you're looking for.

🚂 Train from Strasbourg: 45 min direct.

🛁 Baths — The Whole Point

▼
♨️
Friedrichsbad

The bucket list bathhouse. A 19th-century palatial spa built over Roman springs. No swimwear — the 17-stage bathing ritual (hot rooms, warm rooms, brushdown massage, cool plunge, rest) takes about 3 hours. Mark Twain wrote about this place. Tattoo policy: accepted.

♨️
Caracalla Spa

The modern complement — same thermal water, swimwear-friendly. Indoor and outdoor pools, grottos, saunas, relaxation area. More accessible if Friedrichsbad's nudity requirement feels like a lot. Tattoo policy: welcoming.

🥐 Bakeries & Food

▼
🥐
Black Forest bakeries

Baden-Baden sits at the edge of the Schwarzwald. Schwarzwälder Kirschtorte (Black Forest cake), dense rye breads, cream-heavy pastries. The quality is high and the portions are serious.

🥾 Hiking

▼
🌲
Black Forest trails

Trails start directly from the edge of Baden-Baden. The Merkur mountain (668m) is a 2-hour return hike with panoramic views, or take the funicular up and walk down.

⛰️
Merkur Mountain

The local peak above Baden-Baden. Funicular runs year-round. Walk up through beech forest, take the funicular down, stop at the spa on the way back.

Salzburg
Wednesday–Friday, Days 7–9 🌙🌙🌙

Austria's most beautiful city and most manageable. Compact, walkable, set at the foot of the Alps with the Salzach river running through its centre. The Baroque old town is UNESCO-listed, the food markets are excellent, and Bad Gastein — one of Europe's most dramatic thermal spa towns — is a short train ride south.

🚂 Train from Baden-Baden via Munich: ~3h.

🥐 Bakeries & Food

▼
🥐
Kipferl, Semmel & Germknödel

Austrian bakery culture is exceptional. Kipferl are crescent rolls (the croissant's ancestor), Semmel are soft white rolls. Germknödel are steamed dumplings filled with plum jam and dusted with poppy seed butter — absurdly good.

🍫
Original Mozartkugeln — Fürst

Marzipan and nougat centre, dark chocolate shell. Invented here in 1890. The original Fürst version (silver foil) is sold only at the original shop — completely different from tourist versions.

🛒
Green Market (Universitätsplatz)

Daily outdoor market in the old town. Local cheese, bread, charcuterie, pastries. The best breakfast in Salzburg — eat standing at the stalls.

🛁 Baths

▼
♨️
Bad Gastein — Day Trip ⭐

Don't skip this. A Victorian spa town built vertically into an Alpine gorge with a waterfall crashing down its centre. Radon-rich thermal water, Belle Époque hotels stacked up the cliff walls, outdoor pools steaming against frozen January air. The Felsentherme is the main bath complex. 1.5h south by direct train. Tattoo policy: welcoming.

♨️
Paracelsusbad, Salzburg

Salzburg's own thermal pool complex. Indoor and outdoor pools, sauna. Good for an evening session without the train trip.

🥾 Hiking

▼
⛰️
Untersberg Mountain

Cable car to 1776m, then trails across the Alpine plateau. Views into Bavaria and across the Austrian Alps. Proper January terrain — bring microspikes.

🌊
Bad Gastein Gorge Walk

The waterfall gorge trail is hikeable year-round. Frozen spray on the cliff walls in January. Pair with a Felsentherme session after.

🗺️ Attractions

▼
🏰
Hohensalzburg Fortress

One of Europe's largest and best-preserved medieval fortresses. Take the funicular up, walk down through the old town. Alpine views in winter are outstanding.

🏛️
Getreidegasse & Mozart's Birthplace

The main old town street — unchanged since Mozart's time, lined with wrought-iron guild signs. His birthplace is here. The street is a museum without an entrance fee.

Essential Notes — Trip 1

💶 Currency

France, Germany, and Austria all use Euro. One currency the whole trip.

🌡️ January Weather

Strasbourg and Baden-Baden: 0–5°C, possibly frosty. Salzburg: -2 to 5°C, likely some snow. Bad Gastein: proper Alpine winter. The baths hit differently when it's cold outside.

♨️ Friedrichsbad Booking

Book in advance at carasana.de — it fills up, especially weekends. The nudity aspect is non-negotiable but genuinely not awkward after five minutes.

🚂 Train Booking

France: SNCF Connect app. Germany: DB Navigator app. Austria: oebb.at. Book cross-border tickets on DB Navigator for the Baden-Baden → Salzburg leg.

🥾 Hiking Gear

Microspikes or screw-in boot cleats for January Alpine trails. Not always needed but invaluable if there's been recent snow.

The mountain immersion. After Alsace's wine villages and bakeries, you cross into Switzerland — Bern's medieval arcades and Interlaken's Bernese Oberland Alps — then drop into Innsbruck, the Tyrolean capital ringed tightly by 2000m peaks, with some of Austria's best thermal bath culture nearby.

Strasbourg + Alsace Villages
Friday–Sunday, Days 2–4 🌙🌙🌙

Three days in Alsace. Strasbourg as your base — cathedral, Petite France, bakeries, bouchons, Vosges hiking, municipal thermal baths. A full day on the wine route for village hopping. Same excellent Alsatian content as Trip 1 with an extra day to go deeper.

🚂 Basel → Strasbourg: 1h. Wine villages by rental car.

🥐 Bakeries & Food

▼
🥐
Kougelhopf

The Alsatian crown pastry. Ring-shaped brioche with raisins, almonds, powdered sugar. Every boulangerie. Eat warm with butter.

🥨
Bretzel

The original soft pretzel from Alsace. Lye crust, coarse salt, chewy interior. Pick one up every morning.

🥗
Tarte flambée

Thin crispy dough, crème fraîche, lardons, onion. Order multiple. The Alsatian bouchon staple.

🗺️ Highlights

▼
⛪
Strasbourg Cathedral

Gothic masterpiece, 250 years in the building. Climb to the platform for Rhine valley views.

🏘️
La Petite France

Half-timbered canal district. Empty in January. Extremely beautiful.

🏘️
Wine Villages — Riquewihr, Eguisheim, Kaysersberg

Day trip south along the Route des Vins. Medieval walled villages with excellent pâtisseries. Rent a car for the day.

🛁 Baths

▼
♨️
Les Bains Municipaux

1908 Art Nouveau municipal baths. Hot pools, steam, locals-only vibe. Very affordable.

♨️
Niederbronn-les-Bains

Roman-origin thermal springs 40 min north of Strasbourg. Tiny spa town, completely genuine, almost no tourists in January.

🥾 Hiking

▼
⛰️
Vosges Mountains

30 min west of Strasbourg by car. Forested ridge hiking with castle ruins on the summits. Clear winter views into the Rhine plain.

Bern + Interlaken
Monday–Tuesday, Days 5–6 🌙🌙

Cross into Switzerland. Bern is the Swiss capital and one of Europe's best-preserved medieval cities — six kilometres of covered arcaded walkways line the old town streets, all UNESCO-listed. From Bern, Interlaken is 50 minutes by train and gives access to the Bernese Oberland — the Eiger, Mönch, and Jungfrau directly above. January means snow-capped peaks, few tourists, and excellent bakeries.

🚂 Strasbourg → Basel → Bern: about 2h. Bern → Interlaken: 50 min.

🥐 Bakeries & Food

▼
🥐
Gipfeli

Switzerland's version of the croissant — slightly denser, more buttery. Swiss bakeries open early and the Gipfeli situation is serious.

🧀
Fondue & Raclette

January in Switzerland is peak fondue season. Melted Gruyère in a communal pot, bread dipped in — perfect after a cold morning in the arcades.

🗺️ Attractions

▼
🏛️
Bern's Arcades (Lauben)

Six kilometres of covered stone walkways through the old town, built in the 12th century. The whole city centre is navigable under cover — remarkable urban design that still works perfectly.

🏔️
Interlaken + Jungfrau views

Interlaken sits between two lakes with the Eiger, Mönch, and Jungfrau directly above. January means the peaks are white and the valley is quiet. You don't need to ski to enjoy this.

🥾 Hiking

▼
🌊
Aare River Loop, Bern

Flat riverside path around the Aare peninsula the old town sits on. About 4km, beautiful in winter, views up to the old town walls.

🏔️
Harder Kulm above Interlaken

A ridge above Interlaken with views of all three main Bernese Alps peaks. Funicular goes up, trail comes down. Bring traction devices in January.

Innsbruck
Wednesday–Friday, Days 7–9 🌙🌙🌙

The Tyrolean capital — smaller and more dramatically Alpine than Salzburg. Innsbruck sits in a narrow valley with 2000-metre peaks rising directly above the rooftops. The old town is compact and beautiful, the food market excellent, and the surrounding Tyrol has some of Austria's best thermal bath villages. The cable car from the city centre reaches 2256m in 20 minutes.

🚂 Bern → Zürich → Innsbruck: ~4h. The Arlberg route through the mountains is one of the great European train journeys.

🥐 Bakeries & Food

▼
🥐
Tyrolean Bäckereien

Austrian bakery culture is exceptional. Kipferl, Semmel, and Germknödel — steamed dumplings filled with plum jam, topped with poppy seed and butter. Get the Germknödel.

🥩
Tiroler Gröstl

Diced potatoes and beef pan-fried with onions and caraway, topped with a fried egg. The Tyrolean mountain dish. Perfect after a cold hike.

🛒
Markthalle Innsbruck

Innsbruck's covered food hall. Local cheese, bread, Tyrolean Speck, fresh pasta, pastries. Open weekday mornings. Best breakfast in the city.

🛁 Baths

▼
♨️
Aqua Dome, Längenfeld ⭐

One of the most architecturally striking thermal spas in the world. Three futuristic bowl-shaped outdoor pools perched above an Alpine valley, thermal water steaming into the mountain air. About 1h from Innsbruck. Tattoo policy: welcoming.

♨️
Stubaital Thermal Baths

30 min south of Innsbruck by bus. Well-regarded thermal complex in a ski valley — open to non-skiers.

🥾 Hiking

▼
⛰️
Nordkette Ridge Walk

Cable car from the city centre to 2256m in 20 minutes. Ridge walk across the Hafelekar with views over Innsbruck and the Stubai Alps. Proper Alpine terrain in January — microspikes required.

🌊
Inn River Path

Flat riverside path along the Inn. The view back toward the old town with the mountains behind it is the postcard shot of Innsbruck.

Essential Notes — Trip 2

💶💷 Currency

France and Austria use Euro. Switzerland uses Swiss Franc (CHF) — budget roughly 20–30% more for Swiss days.

🏔️ Alpine Safety in January

The Nordkette above Innsbruck and Harder Kulm above Interlaken are real Alpine environments. Check conditions, bring microspikes, don't push past your comfort level.

♨️ Aqua Dome Booking

Book in advance at aqua-dome.at — popular even in winter. The Ötztal bus connects Innsbruck.

🚂 Switzerland Rail

Swiss trains are famously punctual. Use the SBB app. The Bern → Innsbruck route via Zürich and the Arlberg tunnel is one of the great European train journeys — get a window seat.

Trip 3 — Lyon + Annecy + Salzburg

The food capital route. Fly into Lyon — France's undisputed culinary capital. Then east to Annecy, a town so beautiful it looks painted, on the clearest lake in Europe, ringed by Alps. Then Salzburg for the Austrian finale — architecture, markets, and a day trip to Bad Gastein's thermal gorge. Most food-focused of the three, with equally strong hiking and bath culture.

Fly out: Salzburg (SZG) or Munich (MUC) → PDX

Lyon
Friday–Sunday, Days 2–4 🌙🌙🌙

France's real food capital. The bouchon tradition was invented here, and the city has more great restaurants per capita than anywhere in France. The old town (Vieux-Lyon) is a Renaissance quarter of narrow traboules — hidden passageways through buildings — and stone courtyards. Three days is not too many.

✈️ Arriving via Paris CDG connection or TGV from Paris.

🥐 Bakeries & Food

▼
The Bouchon Tradition
🍽️
Bouchons Lyonnais

The bouchon is Lyon's soul. Small warm bistros serving Lyonnais classics — quenelles de brochet (pike dumplings in cream sauce), salade lyonnaise (frisée with lardons and poached egg), coq au vin. Certified bouchons display an official plaque. Lunch is the main meal.

🧀
Les Halles Paul Bocuse

Lyon's covered food market — named after the city's most famous chef. 60 stalls of exceptional cheese, charcuterie, pastry, fish, and produce. Go weekday mornings. The best food market in France.

Bakeries
🥐
Tarte à la praline

The Lyonnais pastry signature. Shortcrust shell filled with crushed pink pralines that melt into a caramelised filling. Bright pink, slightly alarming, utterly delicious. Every boulangerie in Lyon makes one.

🍩
Bugnes

Lyon's fried dough pastry — thin, crispy, dusted with powdered sugar. A January tradition. Light and addictive. Buy a bag at any bakery.

🗺️ Attractions

▼
🏘️
Vieux-Lyon & Traboules

UNESCO-listed Renaissance old town. The traboules are hidden passageways cutting through buildings from street to courtyard — a secret network built by silk merchants. Many are open to walk through.

⛪
Fourvière Hill & Roman Theatres

19th-century basilica and two well-preserved Roman theatres from the 1st century BC. The views over Lyon in clear January weather are exceptional.

Annecy
Monday–Tuesday, Days 5–6 🌙🌙

Often called the Venice of the Alps — which sounds like tourist copy but is genuinely accurate. Built around canals feeding into the clearest lake in Europe, with the Alps rising on three sides. The old town is small and beautiful, the lake path is one of the great easy walks in France, and the mountains above offer serious hiking. January: crowds completely gone, lake glassy and cold, surrounding peaks snow-covered.

🚂 Train from Lyon: 2h direct.

🥐 Bakeries & Food

▼
🥐
Saint-Genix brioche

The Savoyard pastry signature — brioche studded with pink pralines. Found throughout Annecy's excellent boulangeries. Also look for dense Savoyard rye bread and outstanding croissants.

🧀
Reblochon & Beaufort cheese

The Savoie is serious cheese country. Tuesday and Friday markets in Annecy are outstanding for local producers.

🥔
Tartiflette

Potatoes, Reblochon cheese, lardons, and onions baked until bubbling. The Alpine comfort dish. Every restaurant in Annecy does it.

🗺️ Attractions

▼
🏰
Palais de l'Isle

A 12th-century prison on a triangular island in the main canal. The most-photographed building in Annecy — especially striking reflected in the still winter water.

🌊
Lake Path (Voie Verte)

Flat paved path along the lake edge. In January the light on the water and the snow-capped mountains behind is extraordinary. Bike hire available year-round.

🛁 Baths

▼
♨️
Aix-les-Bains (40 min)

A proper Roman-origin thermal spa town 40 min from Annecy. The Thermes Nationaux has been in continuous operation since the Romans — sulphurous springs, proper spa facilities, beautiful lakeside setting. Worth the detour.

🥾 Hiking

▼
⛰️
Mont Veyrier

2–3 hour return hike directly from town. Views over Lac d'Annecy with the Alps behind in January snow — one of the great views in the Alps. Bring microspikes if there's been recent snow.

🌊
Lake Shore Walk

Walk south from town along the eastern shore, 10km return. Flat, beautiful, extraordinary light in January.

Salzburg
Wednesday–Friday, Days 7–9 🌙🌙🌙

The Austrian finale. Compact Baroque city on the Salzach river, fortress on the hill, the Alps immediately behind. Excellent food markets, world-class patisseries, and Bad Gastein — one of Europe's most dramatic thermal experiences, a Belle Époque spa town built into a steep Alpine gorge — is 1.5h south by direct train.

🚂 Annecy → Geneva → Zürich → Salzburg: ~6h. A spectacular Alpine crossing — get a window seat.

🥐 Bakeries & Food

▼
🥐
Kipferl, Semmel & Germknödel

Austrian bakery culture quietly exceptional. Crescent rolls, soft scored rolls, and steamed dumplings filled with plum jam under poppy seed butter. Morning essentials.

🍫
Original Mozartkugeln — Fürst

The 1890 original — marzipan and pistachio nougat in dark chocolate, silver foil. Only at the original Fürst shop. Completely different from mass-produced tourist versions.

🛒
Green Market (Universitätsplatz)

Daily outdoor market in the old town. Local cheese, bread, charcuterie, pastries. Best breakfast in Salzburg — eat standing at the stalls.

🛁 Baths — Bad Gastein ⭐

▼
♨️
Bad Gastein

Don't miss this. A Victorian and Belle Époque spa town stacked vertically into an Alpine gorge, with a waterfall crashing down its centre. Radon-rich thermal water, the Felsentherme bath complex with outdoor pools directly facing the gorge. In January: snow on the peaks, steam rising from the pools, frozen spray on the cliff walls. Magical. Tattoo policy: welcoming.

♨️
Paracelsusbad, Salzburg

Salzburg's own thermal pool complex for an evening session without the train trip.

🥾 Hiking

▼
⛰️
Untersberg Mountain

Cable car to 1776m, trails across the Alpine plateau. Views into Bavaria and across the Austrian Alps. Microspikes needed on the plateau in January.

🌊
Bad Gastein Gorge Walk

The waterfall gorge trail is hikeable year-round. Frozen spray on the cliff walls in January. Hike in, soak out at the Felsentherme.

🗺️ Attractions

Essential Notes — Trip 3

🍽️ Lyon Bouchon Tips

Certified bouchons display an "Authentique Bouchon Lyonnais" plaque. Lunch is the main meal — some bouchons close early for dinner. Book ahead even in January.

💶 Currency

France and Austria both use Euro. One currency the entire trip.

🚂 The Annecy → Salzburg Train

About 6 hours via Geneva and Zürich — passes through extraordinary Alpine scenery. Get a window seat and don't sleep through it.

♨️ Bad Gastein

Book the ÖBB train from Salzburg in advance at oebb.at. The town is tiny — one main strip of thermal facilities, cafes, and that extraordinary gorge.

🥾 January Hiking Gear

Microspikes for anything above 1500m. Waterproof boots throughout. Layers that compress. The cold is genuinely pleasant when you're dressed for it.
