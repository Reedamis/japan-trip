Osaka
Jan 22–24 · Feb 3–5  ·  🌙🌙 + 🌙🌙
Arrival city · departure city · Japan's food capital
You start and end here. Two nights to settle in at the beginning, two nights to eat everything at the end. Osaka is louder, cheaper, and more fun than Tokyo. The food is the point.
🚆 Getting here: JR Haruka from KIX → Osaka Station ~50 min ~¥1,900. Use ICOCA card for all city travel. For Dotonbori/Namba area: Osaka Metro Midosuji Line.
🍜
Food
▼
Breakfast
🍳
Café Absinthe (カフェ アブサン)
Western-style breakfast, big plates, eggs, bacon, bread. Namba area.
☕
Brooklyn Roasting Company (ブルックリンロースティングカンパニー)
American-style coffee and breakfast. Solid eggs and toast, great espresso.
🍜
Konaya / 今井 (Dotonbori)
Famous Osaka udon institution open for breakfast. Big bowl of thick udon with rice — very filling.
🍱
Traditional Japanese breakfast set (和定食)
Rice, miso, grilled salmon, tamagoyaki, pickles. Look for 朝食 (asa-shoku) in the window.
Lunch
🍢
Daruma (だるま) — Shinsekai
Original kushikatsu chain. Deep-fried skewers — beef, quail egg, lotus root, corn. Dip once, never double-dip.
🥩
Kuromon Ichiba Market (黒門市場)
Graze the stalls — grilled wagyu skewers, tamagoyaki, nikuman pork buns. 150-year-old covered market.
🥟
Gyoza Stadium — Namba Parks (餃子スタジアム)
Entire food court dedicated to gyoza from different regional styles. Order 6 kinds and share.
Dinner
🍜
Kadoya Shokudo (かどや食堂)
60+ year old Bib Gourmand institution. Classic shoyu ramen, thin noodles, bamboo shoots. Not fishy. Get the gyoza.
🍜
Menya Yamahide (麺屋 山秀)
Spicy Taiwan ramen — minced pork, chili, chives. Bold and hearty. The most satisfying bowl in Osaka.
🍜
Zundo-Ya (ずんどう屋)
Rich tonkotsu, multiple locations. Customize broth richness and noodle firmness. Stewed pork platter on the side is mandatory.
🥩
Yakiniku (焼肉) — Namba area
Grilled meat Korean BBQ style. Wagyu gyutan (beef tongue) grilled at the table, cold beer. Great final dinner energy.
Snacks & street food
🐙
Takoyaki (たこ焼き) — Dotonbori
Osaka's signature. Octopus balls, bonito flakes, mayo. Get them hot from a street stall.
🥟
Nikuman (肉まん) — any konbini
Hot steamed pork buns from 7-Eleven or Lawson. Perfect cold morning snack.
🗺️
Attractions
▼
🏮
Dotonbori (道頓堀)
Walk the canal, cross the bridge, walk both sides. The Glico running man, giant crab sign, street performers. More interesting than it sounds.
🏮
Hozenji Yokocho (法善寺横丁)
Tiny stone alley, moss-covered Fudo Myoo statue, paper lanterns. One of the most atmospheric spots in Osaka. 2 min from Kuromon.
🏯
Shinsekai (新世界)
Retro working-class neighborhood built in 1912 to resemble Paris and New York. Arcades, kushikatsu joints, Tsutenkaku Tower.
🏯
Tsutenkaku Tower (通天閣)
Retro tower in Shinsekai. Skip the interior, just walk around it and take in the neighborhood.
🌿
Tennoji Park (天王寺公園)
Free, peaceful, lovely in winter. Walk south from Shinsekai straight into the park.
🪨
Keitaku-en Garden (慶沢園)
Traditional Japanese garden inside Tennoji Park. Free entry. Still water, stone lanterns, bare winter trees.
🏯
Osaka Castle grounds (大阪城)
Skip the interior — just walk the moat and park. The castle is dramatic against a grey February sky. Free to walk.
🌊
Minoh Falls (箕面大滝)
40 min by Hankyu Minoh Line. 2.7km paved trail to a 33m waterfall. Wild macaques in the park. Great if you want fresh air.
🎨
Osaka City Museum of Fine Arts (大阪市立美術館)
Inside Tennoji Park. Strong Japanese and Asian art collection. Free or low entry depending on exhibition.
🎨
Amerikamura (アメリカ村)
Street art, murals, vintage shops. Interesting visually even if you don't shop. 10 min from Namba.
🎭
Entertainment
▼
🐟
Osaka Aquarium Kaiyukan (海遊館)
One of the best aquariums in the world. Massive whale shark tank, mantas, deep-sea creatures. Worth 2–3 hours.
🎮
Namba arcades (ゲームセンター)
Multiple floors of Japanese arcade games — UFO catchers, rhythm games, fighting games. Don't skip the crane games.
🎶
Shinsaibashi (心斎橋)
Covered shopping arcade. Good for picking up Japanese skincare, snacks, quirky souvenirs. People watching is excellent.
🎨
National Museum of Art Osaka (国立国際美術館)
Contemporary international art, underground building. Strong rotating exhibitions. Free on certain days.
☕
Kissaten (喫茶店) — old school coffee shop
A whole culture of slow coffee, thick toast, and boiled eggs. Find one that's been open since the 1970s and just sit for an hour.
🛁
Baths
▼
🛁
Solaniwa Onsen (空庭温泉)
9 spring types, rooftop Japanese garden, torii gate path. Stickers required for communal baths. Private kashikiri baths available ¥4,000–7,000/hr — soak together tattoo-free. Open until 11pm. Bentencho Station.
🛁
Healthy Spa Tateba (ヘルシー温泉タテバ)
Fully tattoo-friendly, no covering. Sign says "Welcome tattoos everyone!" Cold bath, electric bath, jacuzzis, salt sauna. ~¥520. Near Sakuragawa Station.
🛁
Irifune Onsen (入船温泉)
Fully tattoo-friendly, no covering. Beloved neighborhood sento in Nishinari Ward. Electric current bath, totally local. ~¥500.

Kinosaki Onsen
Jan 24–26  ·  🌙🌙
Tiny onsen town · peak crab season · all baths 100% tattoo-friendly
A small town built around hot springs. You check in, get a yukata and wooden geta sandals, and the town is yours. The canal, the bathhouses, the crab — that's Kinosaki. Everything is walkable within 1km.
🚆 Getting here: JR Kounotori Limited Express from Osaka · ~2.5 hrs · Covered by Kansai Wide Area Pass · Reserve seats — fills on weekends.
🍜
Food
▼
Breakfast
🍱
Ryokan breakfast (included)
Your ryokan breakfast is included — rice, miso, grilled fish, tamagoyaki, pickles, tofu. Eat in your yukata. Don't skip it.
Lunch & snacks
🦀
Grilled crab leg street stall
Walk the canal in your yukata, grab a grilled crab leg from a stall. One of those simple perfect moments.
🍡
Canal street stalls
Snacks, local sweets, matcha soft serve, pickles. Graze as you wander.
🍜
Izushi soba (出石そば) — day trip
30 min by bus. Famous soba served in small stacked lacquer dishes. A charming samurai town and a great lunch.
Dinner
🦀
Ryokan kaiseki dinner — Matsuba snow crab (included)
Peak crab season. Multi-course kaiseki dinner included with your stay. Snow crab fished fresh from the nearby Sea of Japan port. Eat in your yukata at a low table. This is the reason people visit Kinosaki in winter.
🗺️
Attractions
▼
🌊
Willow-lined canal (柳並木)
The heart of Kinosaki. Walk it in your yukata, especially at night when the lanterns are lit. Wooden sandals on cobblestones is the sound of this town.
🚡
Kinosaki Ropeway — Mt. Daishi (城崎ロープウェイ)
Take the ropeway up for panoramic views of the Sea of Japan. Walk the descent trail back down through mountain cedar forest.
⛩️
Onsenji Temple (温泉寺)
Ancient temple on the descent path from Mt. Daishi. 500 mossy stone steps through old-growth cedar. Said to have founded Kinosaki's hot springs 1,300 years ago.
🏯
Izushi castle town (出石) — optional day trip
30 min by bus. Snow-dusted samurai castle town with covered walkways and a working clock tower. Famous for Izushi soba.
🎭
Entertainment
▼
🎎
Willow craft shops & pottery
Kinosaki is known for willow craft goods and local pottery. The covered shopping street has small artisan shops worth browsing.
📮
Onsen passport stamp collecting
Your ryokan provides an onsen passport. Collect stamps at each bathhouse. Low-key satisfying.
🦀
Morning market (朝市)
Small local market some mornings near the station. Fresh seafood, local produce, snacks.
🛁
Baths
▼
💡 All 6 public bathhouses are 100% tattoo-friendly — no covering required anywhere. Your ryokan pass covers all of them. Pick one per evening, soak for 30–40 minutes, then you're done for the night.
⭐
Gosho no Yu (御所の湯)
The crown jewel. Only fully all-outdoor rotenburo in Kinosaki — three-tiered pools facing two cascading waterfalls, open sky above. Save for a special evening. Closed Thursdays.
🛁
Ichino Yu (一の湯)
Built into a cave face. The most dramatic architecture in Kinosaki. Great first-night choice.
🛁
Mandara Yu (まんだら湯)
Oldest bathhouse in Kinosaki. Small, intimate, deeply traditional.
🛁
Satono Yu (さとの湯)
Most modern, largest facility, multiple bath types including sauna. Good if you want variety in one visit.
🛁
Yanagi Yu (柳湯)
Named for the weeping willow outside. Compact and beautiful.
🛁
Jizo Yu (地蔵湯)
Patron deity of bathers. Lovely tiled exterior.
🔒
Morizuya Inn private cave onsen (森津屋旅館)
Your ryokan's private cave onsen — book for a time slot to soak together with no separation. Free for guests outside regular hours.

Kyoto
Jan 26–30  ·  🌙🌙🌙🌙
Temples · ramen · hiking · the real Japan
Four nights — enough to move slowly. Kyoto rewards wandering. Go early to the famous spots, spend afternoons in neighborhoods. The ramen scene is excellent and distinctive from Osaka's.
🚆 Getting here: JR Kounotori from Kinosaki → Kyoto · ~2.5 hrs · Covered by Kansai Wide Area Pass. City travel: Kyoto City Bus (¥230 flat fare, ICOCA) or subway.
🍜
Food
▼
Breakfast
☕
% Arabica — Arashiyama (% アラビカ京都 嵐山)
Riverside location, stunning espresso. Best morning coffee of the trip. Go early before the bamboo grove gets crowded.
🍱
Isetan depachika — Kyoto Station (伊勢丹 地下)
Department store basement food hall. Prepared food counters, bento boxes, pastries. Genuinely great low-effort breakfast.
🍵
Kissaten morning set (喫茶店モーニング)
Old-school Japanese coffee shops serve a "morning set" — thick toast, soft boiled egg, coffee for around ¥500. Find one near your hotel.
Lunch
🍜
Omen Nanzenji (おめん 南禅寺)
Handmade udon in rich broth with toppings on the side. Hearty and warming. A Kyoto institution near Philosopher's Path.
🏮
Nishiki Market (錦市場)
Kyoto's 400-year-old covered market. Pickles, tofu, fresh yuba, grilled skewers, matcha everything. Go hungry, graze.
🍡
Tofu kaiseki set lunch
Many Kyoto restaurants offer affordable kaiseki-style set lunches. Silken tofu, mountain vegetables, rice, miso. Lighter than the full dinner version but filling.
Dinner — ramen focus
🍜
Kamodashi Chukasoba ROKU (鴨だしラーメン ROKU)
Kyoto-style ramen by a Michelin-trained chef. Refined but hearty chicken broth. One of the best bowls in the city.
🍜
Menya Inoichi Hanare (麺屋猪一 離れ)
Delicate but deeply flavourful chicken and clam broth. Unusual and memorable. One of Kyoto's most talked-about bowls.
🍜
Takayasu (高安)
Old-school Kyoto-style shoyu ramen, been open since the 1940s. Dark soy broth, thin noodles, green onion. Deeply local.
🍺
Izakaya (居酒屋) evening
Japanese pub with food. Edamame, karaage, yakitori, gyoza, cold beer. Look for a red lantern (赤ちょうちん) outside.
Snacks
🍵
Matcha soft serve
Available everywhere on Nishiki Market and near tourist areas. Deep, bitter, excellent.
🍡
Yatsuhashi (八ツ橋)
Kyoto's signature sweet — thin cinnamon rice crackers, or the soft raw version filled with red bean. Available everywhere.
🗺️
Attractions
▼
🎋
Arashiyama Bamboo Grove (嵐山竹林)
Go at 8–9am — nearly empty in late January. Towering green stalks in winter light. Combine with Tenryu-ji temple garden next door.
🌿
Philosopher's Path (哲学の道)
2km canal-side walkway connecting Ginkaku-ji and Nanzen-ji. Quiet in January, small cafes and galleries along the way.
⛩️
Fushimi Inari (伏見稲荷大社)
Thousands of torii gates winding up a mountain. Go late afternoon when crowds thin. Upper sections in January are often misty and atmospheric. Free entry.
⛩️
Kurama-dera Temple → Kibune hike (鞍馬寺)
Hike up through the ancient mountain temple, traverse the ridge to Kibune valley. Moderate, 3–4 hrs, possibly icy in January. Eizan Railway from Demachiyanagi (~¥430, ICOCA).
⛩️
Nanzen-ji (南禅寺)
One of Kyoto's great Zen temples. Free to wander the grounds. The brick aqueduct running through it is a strange and beautiful surprise.
🌿
Gion district (祇園)
Walking it early morning in winter is its own thing. Historic ochaya (teahouses), stone-paved Hanamikoji street. Best before 9am.
🏮
Nishiki Market (錦市場)
400-year-old covered market street. Good for a slow wander and grazing. Gets crowded by midday.
🎭
Entertainment
▼
🐒
Iwatayama Monkey Park (岩田山モンキーパーク)
20-min hike up. At the top you're inside a wire-mesh hut — ~120 wild macaques roam free outside. You hand food through the wire. Ethical, no chains. ¥800 each.
🎨
Hosoo Gallery (細尾ギャラリー)
Traditional Nishijin textiles as high art. Tiny and beautiful. A very Kyoto experience.
🎨
Kyoto National Museum (京都国立博物館)
Strong Japanese art and artifacts. Rotating special exhibitions. Worth 1–2 hours if you like art history.
🎋
Tenryu-ji garden (天龍寺)
UNESCO World Heritage garden right next to the bamboo grove. One of Japan's greatest gardens — raked gravel, pond, Arashiyama mountain backdrop. ~¥500.
🏯
Nijo Castle (二条城)
Tokugawa shogunate palace with famous "nightingale floors" that squeak with every step — a historic security system. Interesting architecture and gardens.
🛁
Baths
▼
🛁
Kurama Hot Spring (くらま温泉)
Fully tattoo-friendly. Outdoor rotenburo in the mountain forest. Steam rising into cold air. ¥2,500 includes indoor + outdoor. Open until 7pm. Pair with the Kurama-Kibune hike.
🛁
Funaoka Onsen (船岡温泉)
Fully tattoo-friendly, no covering. Built 1923, extraordinary wood carvings throughout. Multiple pools, cold plunge, outdoor bath, sauna. ~¥550. Opens 3pm weekdays (8am Sundays). Closed Tuesdays.

Kumano Kodo + Yunomine Onsen
Jan 30–Feb 1  ·  🌙🌙
Ancient pilgrimage trail · oldest onsen in Japan · very remote
Two nights in the mountains of the Kii Peninsula. The Kumano Kodo is a 1,000-year-old pilgrimage route through ancient cedar forest — the route emperors walked. Yunomine is tiny (maybe 30 buildings) but the hot spring is extraordinary. This is the most remote stop on the trip.
🚆 Getting here: JR Kuroshio Limited Express: Kyoto → Osaka → Kii-Tanabe · ~2.5 hrs · Kansai Wide Area Pass. Then local bus to Takijiri trailhead (~45 min). Activate Pass #2 today.
🍜
Food
▼
On the trail
🍱
Pack lunch from konbini
Buy onigiri and hot drinks at a 7-Eleven before the trailhead. There are no shops on the trail itself.
🏠
Minshuku guesthouse dinner (民宿)
Small family-run guesthouses along the route serve simple hearty dinners — rice, miso, mountain vegetables, grilled fish. Book ahead as options are limited.
In Yunomine
🍱
Ryokan dinner at Yunomine
Most accommodation in Yunomine includes dinner. Mountain vegetables, river fish, miso. After a full day hiking this is exactly what you want.
🥚
Onsen-boiled eggs
The spring water at Yunomine is so hot you can cook in it. Eggs are sold to boil in the stream. Very local experience.
🗺️
Attractions
▼
🌲
Kumano Kodo — Nakahechi route (中辺路)
The main pilgrimage route. Towering ancient cedars, moss-covered stone steps, river gorges, shrine gates appearing from nowhere. February: near-zero crowds, ume blossoms beginning. 2-day walk from Takijiri to Hongu.
⛩️
Kumano Hongu Taisha (熊野本宮大社)
One of Japan's most sacred shrines, deep in the mountains. Arrive on foot the way pilgrims have for a thousand years.
⛩️
Oyunohara (大斎原)
The old site of Hongu Taisha — now a field with a massive torii gate standing alone. Strange and powerful.
🎭
Entertainment
▼
🌸
Ume blossom watching (梅)
Late January into February brings plum blossoms through the forest — white dapples in the cedars. Considered in ancient times more important than cherry blossoms.
🥚
The boiling stream at Yunomine
The stream running through the village is hot spring water. Watch it steam, cook an egg in it, just sit beside it. Genuinely unusual.
🛁
Baths
▼
⭐
Tsuboyu (つぼ湯) — Yunomine Onsen
The only UNESCO World Heritage listed onsen in the world. A tiny cave bath — book by the hour at the public bath counter and have it to yourselves. Water changes color through the day. Check tattoo policy when booking.
🛁
Yunomine public bath (湯の峰温泉 共同浴場)
Small public bath in the village. One of the oldest hot springs in Japan — pilgrims have soaked here for over 1,000 years. Check tattoo policy on arrival.

Kobe → Arima Onsen
Feb 1–3  ·  🌙🌙
Port city · Mt. Rokko hike · golden and silver springs
Kobe for a morning — distinctive from other Japanese cities, with its foreign merchant history and Chinatown. Then hike over Mt. Rokko and descend directly into Arima Onsen on the other side. Arrive sore and very happy.
🚆 Getting here: Bus: Yunomine → Shingu Station · JR Kuroshio → Shin-Kobe · ~3 hrs · Covered by Kansai Wide Area Pass.
🍜
Food
▼
Breakfast
🥟
Nankinmachi morning (南京町)
Kobe's Chinatown opens early. Steamed pork buns (nikuman), char siu, dumplings from street stalls. A different kind of breakfast.
🍱
Pack lunch for Rokko hike
Buy onigiri and hot drinks before heading to the trailhead. You'll be on the mountain all day.
Lunch & dinner
🥩
Kobe beef teppanyaki (神戸牛 鉄板焼き)
You're in Kobe. Lunch sets are more affordable than dinner — around ¥3,000–5,000 for a proper Kobe beef set.
🍜
Ramen Kokoro (ラーメン こころ)
Kobe-style thick soy broth, hearty and rich. Very different from Kyoto's refined bowls.
🍱
Ryokan kaiseki dinner — Arima
Multi-course traditional dinner included with your stay. Arima mountain vegetables, tofu, grilled fish, miso, rice. Eat in your yukata. After the hike you will sleep like rocks.
🗺️
Attractions
▼
🏔️
Mt. Rokko traverse → Arima Onsen (六甲山)
Start at Ashiyagawa trailhead. Rocky sections with fixed chains, 4–5 hrs, panoramic views of Kobe and Osaka Bay. Possible frost in February. Descend directly into Arima Onsen. Most satisfying hike of the trip.
🏯
Kitano Ijinkan (北野異人館)
Hillside district of 19th-century Western-style mansions built by foreign merchants. Strangely beautiful, very quiet in February.
🥟
Nankinmachi — Kobe Chinatown (南京町)
Compact and lively. Street stalls with pork buns, dumplings, char siu. Great for a morning wander before the hike.
🌿
Arima Onsen town wander
Stone-paved streets, small temples, sake shops, toy museum. A few hours of wandering before the train back.
🎭
Entertainment
▼
🎪
Kobe Oji Zoo (王子動物園)
Compact, well-regarded zoo. Has giant pandas. Good if you have extra time in Kobe.
🎨
Hyogo Prefectural Museum of Art (兵庫県立美術館)
Ando Tadao-designed building on the waterfront. Strong modern art collection. The building itself is worth seeing.
🍶
Arima sake and craft shops
The town has small sake breweries and craft shops. Good for picking up a bottle or local souvenirs before leaving.
🛁
Baths
▼
🛁
Kin no Yu — Golden Spring (金の湯)
Fully tattoo-friendly. Iron-rich, rust-brown mineral water. One of Japan's most distinctive hot springs. ~¥800. Open until 9pm. Arrive straight off the mountain.
🛁
Gin no Yu — Silver Spring (銀の湯)
Fully tattoo-friendly. Different mineral profile, lighter-colored water. ~¥550. 10-min walk from Kin no Yu. Do both in the same evening.
🔒
Private kashikiri bath — Negiya Ryofukaku or Gekkoen Korokan
Both offer private rental baths for tattooed guests — ¥3,000–5,000 per 45–60 min session. Soak together, no stickers. Book ahead.

Essential notes
🎫
Kansai Wide Area Pass (5-day, ¥12,000/person) × 2 per person. Activate #1 on Jan 24 (Kinosaki train). Activate #2 on Jan 30 (Kumano train). Buy in advance via Klook or JR West official site before Portland.
💳
ICOCA card — pick up at KIX on arrival. Load ¥10,000–15,000. Works on every subway, bus, local train, and convenience store.
🛁
Tattoo summary: Solaniwa — stickers for communal, private kashikiri available. Tateba + Irifune (Osaka), Funaoka + Kurama (Kyoto), Kin no Yu + Gin no Yu (Arima), all 6 Kinosaki bathhouses — fully tattoo-friendly, no covering needed.
👘
Kinosaki ryokan — book ASAP. Morizuya Inn (morizuya.com) — 4.8 stars, 8 generations family-owned, private cave onsen. February is peak crab season and fills months ahead.
🥾
Hiking boots — Waterproof, mid-height, Vibram soles. Salomon X Ultra 5 Mid GTX (~$190) or Merrell Moab 3 Mid WP (~$130). Break them in well before January.
🧥
Cold weather — Osaka/Kyoto: 3–8°C. Kinosaki (Sea of Japan coast): 2–5°C, possible snow. Kumano (Kii Peninsula): milder, 5–10°C. Pack serious insulated jacket, thermals, hat, gloves, wool socks.
💴
Cash — Japan is largely cash-based outside cities. Carry ¥20,000–30,000 at all times. 7-Eleven and Japan Post ATMs accept foreign cards.
🍜
Ramen cheat sheet: Tonkotsu = rich pork bone broth (milky, hearty). Shoyu = soy sauce broth (lighter, not fishy). Miso = fermented soybean (earthy, warming). Spicy tantanmen = sesame + chili. Start with shoyu or tonkotsu.
